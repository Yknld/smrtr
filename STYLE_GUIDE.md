# Educational Motion Graphics Style Guide

## Core Principles

**Premium educational motion graphics** focused on clarity, professionalism, and effective learning.

## Visual Style

### Background
- **Base**: Neutral grey background
- **Gradient**: Subtle gradient overlay (not dramatic)
- **Purpose**: Provides calm, unobtrusive foundation for content

### Design Aesthetics
- **Style**: Modern flat/vector look
- **Approach**: Minimal clutter
- **Priority**: High readability above all else
- **Complexity**: Keep designs simple and clean

### Restrictions (Do NOT Include)
- ❌ No emojis
- ❌ No logos
- ❌ No brand names
- ❌ No watermarks
- ❌ No text baked into images (text will be overlaid in post-production)

### Motion & Timing
- **Pacing**: Calm classroom pacing
- **NOT**: Cinematic timing (too slow/dramatic)
- **NOT**: TikTok-style (too fast/frenetic)
- **Feel**: Professional, educational, measured progression

## Technical Specifications

### Color Palette (Suggested)
- **Base Grey**: `#F5F5F5` to `#E8E8E8` range
- **Gradient**: Subtle shift (5-10% variation)
- **Accent Colors**: TBD per content needs (maintain high contrast)

### Animation Principles
- Smooth, deliberate movements
- Clear transitions between concepts
- Pause time for comprehension
- Consistent easing curves

## Content Guidelines

1. **Visual Hierarchy**: Use scale, position, and opacity to guide attention
2. **Information Density**: One concept per scene/segment
3. **Duration**: Allow sufficient time for learners to process information
4. **Consistency**: Maintain visual language throughout series

## Text Overlay (Post-Production)
- All text will be added in post-production
- Design graphics to accommodate text areas
- Leave safe zones for text placement
- Consider text legibility in design choices
